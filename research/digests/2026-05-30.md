## Finding 1 — Competitors run a fully-scripted DM/chatting *system*; we have none documented

**This is our single biggest blind spot.** Multiple operators treat chatting as a formal,
teachable sales process with defined phases, fan tiering, and scripts — the way a real sales
org runs. We describe our chat team only as "all-female, trained on the creator's voice"
(baseline §5 = **GAP, no internal baseline yet**).

**What they do:**
- **3-phase sales funnel** — Intro (build rapport: name, location, job, hobbies; make them feel
  they're talking to a real person) → Building (escalate sexual tension, tease content) →
  Selling (PPV with manufactured urgency: "only available 10 minutes," "only a couple left").
  *habibi runs this exact system across "multiple 7-figure accounts."*
- **Fan tiering** — explicitly classify every fan as whale / mid-tier / low-spender and tailor
  approach to each.
- **A repeatable 4-part script** (greeting → qualification → … ) credited with **$50K in 30
  days**; the qualification step exists specifically to gauge how much a fan will spend.

**How it differs from us:** We have no documented funnel, qualification step, fan-tiering model,
PPV scripting, or urgency framework. Our chatters are trained on "voice" — tone — but there's no
captured *sales methodology* behind the monetization that actually drives OF revenue.

**What we could change:** Build a Palm chatting SOP: a named funnel, a fan-tier model wired to
our existing Fan Tracker / whale-hunting data (we already capture whale data — §10/website), and
a baseline PPV script with a qualification step. This is also the first **gap** to fill in the
baseline doc.

**Sources:** habibi, *The Complete A-Z OnlyFans Chatting Playbook* — funnel [0:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o_Gs5DY8PzY&t=30s), fan tiers [later] · Luca Pritchard, *This Chatting Script Made $50,000 in 30 Days* — [0:30](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7hC3TsQ1lqg&t=30s)

## Finding 2 — "Productized content sets" vs. our bespoke inspo pipeline

Hive Media ($750K–$1M/mo, 60 models, 300 staff) runs content as a **standardized factory**, not
a per-creator custom build. This is the sharpest contrast with our approach (baseline §2: our
inspo→film→edit→post pipeline is data-driven but bespoke per creator).

**What they do:**
- Every model delivers **2 "sets" per week**, ~2 hrs to shoot each. A set is rigidly structured:
  **Part A** SFW/realism (20–30 pics + 10–15 short clips), **Part B** sexy-compliant (IG/X/Reddit
  thirst traps), **Part C** the selling layer (a sexting set or PPV).
- A **character sheet** per model (occupation, personality, persona, fan demo) created at
  signup; all content + captions + PPVs align to it. They explicitly target fan psychology by
  look ("petite → white-knight/sugar-daddy fans"; "MILF → struggling-single-mom angle").
- Content is shot once, then **broken into distribution folders** (profile pics, sexy, NSFW
  teasers, sexting, PPV) feeding both the marketing machine and the sales page.
- Their stated constraint: models work **4–6 hrs/week** — the whole system is built around minimal
  creator time.

**How it differs from us:** We brief creators per-reel off an inspo board (quality-first, custom).
That's a genuine strength — but it's higher-touch and harder to scale than a fixed 2-sets/week
"box." We have no standardized weekly set spec, and our character/persona work (we *do* have
Palm Creators "content pillars / brand voice," §2) isn't tied to a fixed content-template output.

**What we could change:** Pilot a standardized weekly "set" spec for a subset of creators on top
of our inspo system — keep the data-driven idea selection, but package required output into a
repeatable A/B/C deliverable so production scales with less per-creator overhead. Our AI personas
(§0) are the ideal first test: no real-person scheduling friction.

**Sources:** faceless francis, *How a $1M/Month OFM Agency Actually Works* — set format [34:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=2065s), A/B/C breakdown [35:28](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=2128s), character sheet [38:37](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=2317s), distribution folders [45:49](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=2749s)

## Finding 3 — They reverse-engineer revenue with a financial model and "revenue quality"; we track invoices, not unit economics

DylanOFM's whole "$1M/month blueprint" is operator discipline, and two pieces are directly
actionable for us. Baseline §1/§6: we set commission at onboarding and compute weekly invoices,
but have no documented financial model or per-creator economics.

**What they do:**
- **Reverse-engineer the revenue goal:** start from target cash/month → required net signings
  (after churn) → close rate → calls booked → outbound volume. Track *contribution margin after
  direct labor*, 30-/90-day cash-collected per creator, churn, time-to-first-result.
- **"Not all revenue is good revenue":** select/cut creators on contribution margin, attention
  load, churn risk, coachability, and content volume — not gross inflow. A cooperative 30K/mo
  creator at 80% margin beats a 500K/mo one at 2% that needs daily founder hand-holding.
- **CEO scorecard / operating rhythm:** every function measurable (acquisition, sales, delivery
  KPIs) on one dashboard; weekly leadership cadence; "no hiding behind effort language."

**How it differs from us:** We have invoicing and commission %, but no documented
contribution-margin-per-creator view, no creator-selection scorecard, and no single ops
dashboard of acquisition→sales→delivery KPIs.

**What we could change:** Define a Palm creator-unit-economics model (margin after chat/edit/
traffic labor, 30/90-day cash, churn) and a lightweight creator-selection scorecard. We already
have the data spine (Airtable: invoices, creators, posts) — this is largely a reporting layer,
plausibly a future module on the admin dashboard (§ website dashboard).

**Sources:** DylanOFM, *How I Helped 12 OFM Agencies Hit $1M/Month* — financial model [23:25](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=118P4rIlfl0&t=1405s), reverse-engineering [24:59](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=118P4rIlfl0&t=1499s), revenue quality [33:45](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=118P4rIlfl0&t=2025s), scorecard/operating rhythm [43:38](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=118P4rIlfl0&t=2618s)

## Finding 4 — PPV pricing is a discipline (anchoring, escalation, tip menus); we have no pricing model

Two operators lay out a concrete PPV pricing method. Baseline §6 = **GAP, no internal baseline
yet** — we have no documented pricing/monetization strategy at all, despite commission being the
#1 creator concern in our own market research.

**What they do:**
- **A concrete escalation ladder within one session:** first PPV $15 → $25 → $45 → $65 → then
  $100–$120 once they've bought the lower tiers. Next session, *nudge the opener up* ($15→$19) to
  test the ceiling per fan. (Luca's agency: $150K/mo, "most from PPVs in chat.")
- **Start low to build the buying habit** — first-ever PPV $5–$20 so the sub gets used to
  unlocking; never open a new sub at $50.
- **Question-caption tactic** — caption the PPV as a question he can't answer without unlocking
  ("do you like how I do this?") — assumptive close, not begging.
- **Premium pricing / no "flea-market" odd numbers** — habibi argues *against* the standard
  $9.99/$13.37 gimmicks and mass-message bundle dumps: price with creator-confidence, kill mass
  messages entirely (they burn out the fan base and tank LTV).
- **Tip menus** — a structured menu of priced actions as a monetization surface.

**How it differs from us:** Nothing documented on our side — no anchoring, escalation ladder, or
tip-menu standard. This is purely a learn-from-others item right now.

**What we could change:** Draft a Palm PPV/tip-menu pricing playbook (anchor + escalation ladder
+ standard tip menu), and fold pricing into the chatting SOP from Finding 1. Fills baseline §6.

**Sources:** Luca Pritchard, *How to Price Your PPV* — escalation ladder $15→120 + test-the-opener [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JXLdjNmHL-8&t=60s) · Luca Pritchard, *Chatting Script $50K/30 days* — first PPV $5–20 + question-caption [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7hC3TsQ1lqg&t=60s) · habibi — anti-mass-message + premium pricing [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=o_Gs5DY8PzY) · Henry Hien, *PPV Pricing That Gets Fans to Buy* [video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rhxBUvXYQ8Y&t=30s)

## Finding 5 — Account scale & "trust score" creation vs. our single-persona warmup

Hive's distribution model is radically more aggressive than ours, and their account-creation
methodology is more developed. Baseline §4: we run a strong hardware-isolated **90-day warmup**,
but per-persona (one identity stack per AI account), not mass multi-account distribution.

**What they do:**
- **200 social accounts per model** at signup (100 active, 100 reserve) across IG/X/Reddit/Threads;
  ~10,000 accounts maintained globally, with an expected burn/ban rate designed in.
- **"Trust score" creation discipline:** 100% mobile-created on fresh devices, high-authority
  emails (Gmail/iCloud > Proton), **private** (not mainstream) proxies, mobile proxy for creation
  + residential to run, 1 proxy per account per platform to avoid chain-bans.
- **Reddit as a CPA channel:** treated as paid acquisition — **$0.25–$1 cost per free subscriber,
  $4–$8 return**, ~100 manual Reddit VAs, performance tracked daily per VA.
- Instagram/X/Threads heavily **automated** (phone farms + scripting); Reddit kept manual/skill.

**How it differs from us:** Our warmup is deep but single-stream per persona; we don't run a
multi-account-per-creator distribution model, a documented trust-score creation standard beyond
the AI-persona playbook, or Reddit-as-CPA with per-VA economics. Our GrapheneOS/Pixel warmup
(§4) is arguably *higher* integrity per account — the lesson is breadth + ban-resilience, not
replacing our method.

**What we could change:** Consider a reserve-account / multi-account strategy for ban resilience
on our AI personas, and evaluate Reddit as a measured CPA channel (it maps cleanly to our gap in
traffic, §8). Adopt the trust-score creation checklist where it strengthens our warmup playbook.

**Sources:** faceless francis, *How a $1M/Month OFM Agency Actually Works* — 200 accounts/model [1:33](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=93s), trust-score creation [4:41](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=281s), private proxies [8:52](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=532s), Reddit CPA/LTV [31:19](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=bklo6HZBxRY&t=1879s)
