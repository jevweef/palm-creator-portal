## 0. Credibility frame (read first)

Dylan is a guru with a **small YouTube footprint (~8.4k subs) and very large self-reported revenue
claims** — $1.6M/mo agency, 60% margins, "551 agencies installed," a creator at $282K/mo. None of it
is independently verifiable; the on-screen "proof" is screenshots he controls, and at least one
headline number is cherry-picked (the $118K welcome-message figure is qualified as "the first 11
days of July," [19:14]). Treat the **dollar claims as marketing** and the **framework as the
signal** — the framework is internally coherent and overlaps with higher-consensus findings already
in Palm's knowledge base (productize, fan/whale prioritization, paid-traffic acquisition).

This is also a **pitch video**: the whole thing funnels to a paid workshop / booking call ([31:16]–
[32:18]). He withholds the actual mechanics — the welcome-message copy, the full chat "drip sets,"
the deal-structure math — because those are the paid product. So what we can reconstruct is the
**skeleton and the numbers he chose to flash**, not the muscle.

**One thing that modestly *raises* credibility (from the vision pass):** the OnlyFans earnings
statements on screen are real exports dated **Feb–July 2025** — i.e. this is a genuine ~14-month-old
case study, not numbers invented on the spot ([12:40], [4:41]; Appendix A 02–03). They're still *his
own* screenshots and unaudited, but they're dated, itemized OF statements, not round-number flexes.

## 1. TL;DR

**His one idea:** *productized fulfillment* — run the **exact same delivery system for every
creator** and let "the creator be the variable" ([1:33]–[2:05]). Same onboarding, same content
verticals, same funnel, same welcome message, same chat strategy, same posting schedule. You don't
invent anything per-creator; you sign avatar-fit creators, plug them into one machine, and outcomes
spread (44K/mo, 100K/mo, 280K/mo) off **identical effort** ([7:16]–[7:47]).

**The machine is one hub-and-spoke map he calls "The Creator Growth Engine"** (on-screen, [15:37];
Appendix A 04), with a sticky note reading *"Every top 1% creator we work with has these 4 levers
installed."* The four levers + a scaling layer:
1. **Viral Hook Engine™** — fixed content "verticals" (one is on-screen as *"Viral Clip Split"*; he
   says *Omegle* + *talking head*), brute-force cadence (6 IG posts/day, 20 trial reels/day), 1–2 IG
   + 1–2 TikTok accounts ([16:08]–[18:44]).
2. **Funnel Optimisation** — link-in-bio (link.me), one standardized welcome message ("generates
   $30k/mo in unlocks"), an OF sub promo ($14.99 + 75%-off + "3 spots left" scarcity), and conversion
   profile videos for the ~50% of subs who buy without chatting ([18:44]–[20:48]).
3. **Whale & High Spender Retention + AI Optimisation** — heavy AI (voice cloning, $100 AI voice
   notes baked into "drip sets"), whale-first priority messaging by location/time-zone with
   unsend-and-retry, personalized whale thank-you videos, a Fan Spending Tracker ([20:48]–[22:51]).
4. **Chat Monetization** — the revenue layer the above feed; on the OF statements, the *Messages*
   line is where the growth actually lands (see §2.11).
5. **Scale With Systems™ / Ascension** — *after* the creator is winning, upsell into paid **clipping**
   (40¢ /1k views, ~$20K/mo, creator pays) + a Main→Backup IG/TT account infrastructure ([22:51]–[24:54]).

**Deal + onboarding:** **$5K upfront + 50%** rev-share, and onboarding is defined as "**complete only
after the first full week of service delivery**," not at signing ([13:32], [14:34]).

**The single biggest takeaway for Palm:** Palm already *has* the rare half of this (a productized,
data-driven content engine — §1). What Dylan productizes that Palm has **left ad-hoc or outsourced**
is the **monetization half**: a standardized welcome message, standardized conversion videos, a
standardized whale-priority chat playbook, and a deal structure that's profitable on day one. That's
exactly the gap the mentor report already flags (§7 pricing, §8 chatting, §9 retention) — this video
is a concrete, end-to-end example of an operator who closed it.

## 2. The reconstructed operating system

### 2.1 Core thesis — productized fulfillment, creator as the variable

- [1:01] Target pain: agencies stuck between **$15K–$150K/mo** that "fail to productize."
- [1:33]–[2:05] *Productized fulfillment = the same delivery model for every creator.* Standardize
  "the onboarding, the content cadence, the retention flows, the chatting strategies, the posting
  schedules — everything." Then "grab this creator, plug them into the machine," and every creator
  runs the same flow, coming out with different results.
- [2:05]–[3:39] The anti-pattern he's selling against: **single-creator dependency.** "It is
  inevitable… you will get that breakup text and the creator is going to leave." She leaves for an
  agency that "undercuts you by 5%," or because she's big enough to go solo, or "she renegotiates you
  down to 30%" ([3:08]). Claims he's "worked with over 500 agencies" and most hit this.
- [8:49]–[9:21] Why traditional 50/50 revshare can't scale: every creator is unique, so "you can
  never actually train your team on functions and processes that are the same." [9:21] "Most of you
  that have five creators is literally the exact same as you working with five separate complete
  different niche businesses."
- [5:12] Mantra: **"Simplicity scales, complexity fails."**

### 2.2 The ideal avatar (who he signs)

- [3:39]–[4:10] **Hard avatar gate:** "we go after creators where all their traffic is already
  coming from Instagram and TikTok. Nothing else." Must already (a) have a presence, (b) be posting,
  (c) have an OnlyFans, (d) do explicit content.
- [6:16]–[6:46] **How to pick your avatar:** reverse-engineer your best past creator — "what client
  was the easiest to work with, what client had the longest LTV, what client made you the most
  amount of money… and who was the most profitable," then build the whole agency around that profile.
- [24:54]–[25:25] **Creator DNA traits** (the signing scorecard): coachable · consistent · high
  output · "creative bound" (willing to do explicit content) · data-driven · **no entitlement** ·
  willing to invest in their own growth.
- [25:25]–[25:58] **Upfront payment as a filter:** paying to join "acts as a filter for us." Girls
  who won't pay and "just want us to take a percentage" are screened out.
- [26:30]–[27:02] **Effort-precedes-income signal:** the winning creator "acted like a 100K–200K
  creator before she was one — posting 10 times a day even at 10K/mo." Income lagged, then went
  exponential.
- [28:05]–[28:37] **Anti-niche stance:** "you are your own niche." He *scraps* prior "gym niche"
  accounts because niching forces you to "compete on looks." The differentiator from 10K→280K is
  **personality**, and the system's job is to "get the best of your personality" ([28:37]–[29:09]).

### 2.3 Deal structure & onboarding trigger

- [13:32] **The deal: "5K up front, 50%."** "She paid that. We onboarded her into the system and that
  is the first step of the productized fulfillment system. Everything runs automatic… as soon as she
  signs that agreement, it triggers all of this to go off."
- [9:51]–[10:24] He frames the deal structure as **lever #2** (after productizing): "a new deal
  structure beyond just the 50/50 revshare model… that allows us more profit up front, more longer
  LTV." The upfront fee is the profit-on-day-one mechanism (so you're "not reliant on the creator
  paying invoices," [31:46]).
- [22:51]–[23:21] **Leverage to upsell later comes from NOT giving everything away at 50/50 on day
  one:** "you can upsell your creators if you don't give away everything at the start like most
  agencies and have no leverage."

### 2.4 System install → onboarding "complete" definition

- [13:32]–[14:02] After signing, they "do the system install on her account… get everything on
  OnlyFans, everything on social media. This flow runs automatically through either people or
  automations."
- [14:02]–[14:34] **Owner is fully removed:** "I wouldn't even necessarily know that we've signed
  her unless I check our Slack automation… she could be in the agency for 3–4 months… making 280K a
  month because I'm not involved at all." (This is the productization payoff — and also the part he
  can't prove.)
- [14:34]–[15:07] **Key operational definition:** "**Onboarding is only complete once the first week
  of full service delivery has taken place** — the first week of chatting, the first week of posting…
  Onboarding is *not* complete when they have signed the agreement and you've got their account
  details. That is a mistake." Then "the flywheel kicks in" — same weekly system for OF + social.

### 2.5 The Creator Growth Engine — Pillar 1: Viral Hook Engine

- [15:37]–[16:08] The engine has, on-screen, **four parts**: *Viral Hook Engine · Funnel
  Optimization · Scaling with Systems · Chat Monetization*. (He later calls the live revenue parts
  the **"three pillars"** — content, funnel, chat — with **scaling as the layer that comes after**.)
- [16:38]–[17:10] **The verticals (named, not explained):** he plugs every creator into the **"Omegle
  vertical" and "talking head vertical" first.** "These are the same verticals that all of our
  creators get." Transition results for Peachy: 17K → 18K → 5K → 6K → 7K → 53K, and "our sixth video
  got 1.5 million views — more than the entire account prior."
- [18:13]–[18:44] **Evergreen, anti-trend, anti-niche:** "We already know the verticals that work
  every single time… evergreen verticals that never go out of date. We're not putting creators in
  niches… not doing trends. We have scientifically engineered specific verticals that work with every
  avatar-fit creator." (He never lists the full set — this is paid-product withholding.)
- [18:44] **Cadence (specific):** "posting as many times per day as possible — **six times a day on
  Instagram, 20 times per day on trial reels**." Focus on **1–2 Instagram accounts and 1–2 TikTok
  accounts.**
- [17:42]–[18:13] **Volume philosophy:** "quality is… what your audience says it is. You use quantity
  to learn what they think is [quality]." Win on conversion *density*, not raw views ([30:11]: "I
  know people getting 20–30 million views barely scraping 10K/mo").

### 2.6 Pillar 2: Funnel Optimization (traffic → buyer)

- [18:44]–[19:14] **"If you get all this traffic but you have no way to convert it… you may as well
  do fuck all."** Link-in-bio = **Linkme** — "the fastest, super normal bio, super basic offer that
  we run for everyone… just proven."
- [19:14]–[19:46] **One standardized welcome message** for nearly every creator: "we generated
  $118,000 from a single welcome message with a **30% conversion rate** — this welcome message alone
  generates $30,000 every single month." (Caveat in his own words: "this only accounts for the first
  11 days of July" — i.e. the $118K is a cherry-picked window.)
- [19:46]–[20:48] **Conversion profile videos** for non-chatters: "average spend per fan is $32…
  every subscriber doesn't want to chat — like 50% — they've gone horny and want to buy something
  straight away. These videos let the subs do that," lifting average spend + conversion.
- [20:48] **"Every top 1% creator has these four levers installed: the funnel setup, the welcome
  message setup, the conversion posts…"**

### 2.7 Pillar 3: Chat Monetization

- [20:48]–[21:18] **AI-heavy:** "we use AI a lot… we do AI voice cloning. The examples are spot on —
  you would not be able to tell they're AI. We're literally selling **AI voice notes for $100** and
  selling a lot of them. Your creator doesn't need to do anything."
- [21:18]–[21:48] **Drip sets / scripts:** personalized voice notes added "to all of our drip sets,
  or you might call them scripts."
- [21:48]–[22:19] **Whale-first priority messaging:** "whales and high-spender attention is our main
  priority. We have priority messaging based on location… mass messages targeting our highest-value
  fans based on their location and time zone… reach them during their most active periods. For
  example, morning before work; **if they don't respond within an hour, the message is unsent**, so
  the follow-up can be sent around when they get off work." Goal: avoid "10 mass messages stacked in
  their inbox" looking generic/spammy.
- [22:19]–[22:51] **Whale care:** "personalized thank-you videos sent to whales… we get our creators
  to do that." Plus a "whale fan spending tracker" and a high-spenders folder.

> **Note on the chat pillar.** This is where Dylan is *thinnest on mechanics and richest on tooling*.
> He gives AI voice cloning, a $100 price point, location-based send timing, and whale prioritization
> — but **no PPV pricing ladder, no objection handling, no pre-sale funnel, no fan-tier taxonomy.**
> Palm's existing knowledge base (mentor report fixes #1–#2) is *more* specific here than this video.
> Use Dylan for the **AI-voice and whale-timing tactics**; use the corpus for the actual chat funnel.

### 2.8 Scaling layer: Ascension (clipping + paid)

- [22:51]–[23:21] Only **after** a creator is winning on the three pillars (he says "maybe 2, 3, 4
  months") do they "ascend them into a more custom delivery."
- [23:21]–[23:53] **Account infrastructure:** main account + "bank accounts" (backups) on IG + TikTok,
  then **clipping accounts** of those pages. First month of clipping: **26M views, 1,080 posts,
  paying 40¢ per 1,000 views.**
- [23:53]–[24:23] **Cost framing:** 40¢/1k views vs "$8 on Instagram ads, $34 on LinkedIn." They
  "spend ~$20K/mo on clipping campaigns once creators have proven they can work." **The creator pays**
  ("not ourselves, but our creators… we take a percentage"), so the agency profits on the spend *and*
  the new traffic.
- [24:23]–[24:54] Crucially: **Peachy hit $280K/mo in 60 days *without* clipping** — clipping is the
  upsell layer *on top of* an already-working productized base.

### 2.9 Acquisition philosophy

- [9:51]–[10:55] Acquisition is **lever #3.** Cold outreach "is volume-based and unstable unless
  you've got an amazing personal brand… most of you outreach from shitty pages… you need to do
  outreach for at least 100 days in a row." **"The biggest lever for scaling acquisition is paid ads."**
- [10:55]–[11:27] "Cold outreach is hunting. 50/50 is a hunting deal structure. Custom delivery is
  hunting. Operators build something that *pulls* — that creates leverage." Claims this took him to
  $1.6M/mo at 60% margins.
- [29:09] **Run it like a talent agency:** "sign as many creators as you possibly can, plug them into
  the same system, let the creators do the work, see the best results, and then ascend." Stated goal
  in the CTA: **"signing three to four new models every single week"** ([31:46]).

## 4. Palm mapping — component by component

For each component: what Dylan does → how Palm does it today (`docs/palm-operating-system.md §N`) →
what to change, with a **real-creator / AI-only / both** tag.

### 4.1 Productized fulfillment (the core idea)
**Dylan:** one identical delivery system; creator is the only variable.
**Palm today:** Palm is *partly* productized — the social content engine is genuinely productized and
data-driven (§1 MATURE), and AI-persona warmup is fully specified (§3/§4). But monetization
(pricing §7, chat §8, retention §9) is *not* productized — it's per-creator/outsourced/ad-hoc.
**Change — both:** Palm already out-productizes Dylan on *content ideation* (its inspo→film→edit→post
engine is more sophisticated than his fixed "verticals"). The asymmetry is the **back half**. Adopt
his framing explicitly: write down a single standardized **monetization delivery spec** (welcome
message, conversion videos, whale rules, PPV ladder) that every creator gets by default — the same
way Palm already standardizes content. This is the through-line of the whole mapping below.

### 4.2 Avatar definition & signing scorecard
**Dylan:** hard gate (IG/TikTok traffic, already posting, has OF, explicit) + a DNA scorecard
(coachable, consistent, high output, data-driven, no entitlement, invests in growth) + upfront fee
as a filter.
**Palm today:** acquisition is Josh-DM-led and ad-hoc; no written fit scorecard, no avatar definition
(§6 THIN — flagged GAP).
**Change — real-creator (today); both (later):** add a written **avatar gate + DNA scorecard** to the
onboarding wizard as a hard signing filter (this echoes mentor-report fix #3 and the Markuss-Hussle
red-flag screen). Dylan's "effort-precedes-income" tell (posts 10×/day *before* she's big) is a
concrete, cheap signal worth adding. For AI personas the avatar is *designed*, so the scorecard
becomes a *build spec* instead of a filter.

### 4.3 Deal structure ($5K upfront + 50%)
**Dylan:** upfront fee → profit on day one + leverage to upsell later; flexes off 50/50 by tier.
**Palm today:** single commission % set per creator at onboarding; weekly auto-invoicing; **no
documented pricing/deal model (§7 THIN — explicitly Palm's #1 unwritten gap).**
**Change — real-creator:** this is the highest-leverage borrow. Palm runs pure rev-share with no
upfront component, which means it carries 100% of setup cost with no floor and has no leverage to
upsell. Pilot an **upfront-setup-fee + rev-share** structure (Dylan's $5K+50%) for *new* signings,
and write the tiered deal matrix the mentor report already recommends (beginner / mid / whale).
**Caveat:** Palm's creators are *acquired*, not buying a course — an upfront fee changes the sales
motion and may not fit Palm's relationship-led signing. Test on one cohort before standardizing.

### 4.4 "Onboarding complete after week 1 of delivery"
**Dylan:** onboarding isn't done at signing — it's done after one full cycle of posting + chatting.
